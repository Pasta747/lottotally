# Blog-to-Social Pipeline — Repeatable Process
**For every Pinger blog post, generate these 5 assets automatically**  
**Owner:** Gary writes → Automation_Poster executes on activation  

---

## THE FORMULA

Every blog post produces:
1. **X/Twitter thread** — 5-6 tweets, numbered, hook → insight → CTA
2. **X standalone tweet** — single best insight, no thread needed
3. **LinkedIn post** — 150-200 words, link post, professional tone
4. **Canopy warmup post** (Canopy blog posts only) — observation, no product mention

---

## THREAD FORMULA (X/Twitter)

**Tweet 1 (hook — make them stop scrolling):**
Start with a scene, a number, or a counterintuitive claim. The reader should immediately think: "this is about me." No product name in Tweet 1. No CTA. Just the hook.

**Tweet 2 (the problem, sharpened):**
Name the pain precisely. One tweet, no more than 3 short paragraphs. What's actually broken?

**Tweet 3 (the insight):**
The non-obvious thing. What most people miss. What you've learned from talking to the ICP. This is the tweet people screenshot and share.

**Tweet 4 (the framework or numbers):**
Give them something structured to hold onto. A list, a stat, a 3-step framework. Tweetable, standalone value.

**Tweet 5 (the implication):**
What this means for their business specifically. Revenue, relationships, time. Make it concrete.

**Tweet 6 (CTA):**
Product mention + link. Keep it short. "We built [X] for [who]. Free to start → link"

**Threading mechanics:** Post Tweet 1, then immediately reply to your own tweet with Tweet 2, etc. This chains them.

---

## STANDALONE TWEET FORMULA

Take the single most shareable insight from the post. Usually:
- A counterintuitive claim
- A before/after scenario
- A number that reframes the problem
- A one-liner that's quotable

No thread. No CTA in the tweet itself (put the link as a reply or leave it out — standalone tweets get more reach without links).

---

## LINKEDIN POST FORMULA

**Opening line:** Strong statement or question. LinkedIn readers see only the first 2 lines before "see more" — make those 2 lines earn the click.

**Body (3-4 short paragraphs):**
- The problem you're solving (1 paragraph)
- The non-obvious insight from your research (1 paragraph)  
- What you built and why it's different (1 paragraph)
- Optional: specific use case or outcome

**CTA line:** One sentence. "Full breakdown at the link." or "Free to try at [link]."

**Formatting:** Short paragraphs. Line breaks between every 1-2 sentences. No bullet points in LinkedIn text — use em dashes or arrows (→) for lists. No markdown.

**Link:** Either in the post text or as a "Link preview" card — test which performs better and standardize.
