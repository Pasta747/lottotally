## 5) Landing Page Copy Review — What Needs Updating

Overall: the landing page is strong. The voice is right. Einstein can use it. But a few areas should be tightened before traffic hits it.

### Keep as-is
- Hero headline: **"Engage with your community on your terms."**
- Core framing around community over victimhood
- Filter mode structure
- FAQ tone
- Visual direction and design notes

### Recommended updates before launch

#### A. Narrow the platform promise in Section 3 + Features
**Issue:** The page currently implies broader support than we may actually have on day one.

**Current risk lines:**
- "Link your YouTube, podcast, or social accounts"
- "YouTube comments, podcast community posts, Instagram — one place to see and engage with it all"

**Why update:** If product support is not fully live across all of those, this creates avoidable trust damage.

**Recommended replacement:**
- In Section 3, Step 1:  
  **"Connect your channels"**  
  "Start with YouTube today. Additional platform support is rolling out next. Canopy begins filtering comments immediately."

- In Features, Multi-platform:  
  **"Multi-platform, rolling out"**  
  "Start with YouTube. Podcast and additional social integrations are on the roadmap and coming next."

#### B. Soften or remove hard setup-time claims unless verified
**Issue:** There are several exact setup-time promises: "Takes two minutes" and "Set it up in five minutes."

**Why update:** Tiny credibility loss if actual setup is slower.

**Recommended replacement:**
- "Set it up once. You'll feel the difference immediately."
- "Connect your account and Canopy starts reading comments right away."

#### C. Weekly digest / response drafts / monthly report may be too fully baked in copy
**Issue:** The landing page reads like every feature is production-ready now.

**Why update:** If any of these are roadmap or partial, label them honestly.

**Recommended fix:** Add light status labels where needed:
- "Weekly community digest"
- "Response drafts in your voice"
- "Monthly community report"

Possible labels:
- **Available at launch**
- **Rolling out in beta**
- **Coming soon**

If Einstein confirms all three are live enough, keep them. If not, label them.

#### D. Pricing is probably premature unless product + billing are truly ready
**Issue:** The pricing table is polished, but specific.

**Why update:** If Stripe/billing/product entitlements aren't ready, the table becomes a liability.

**Recommendation:**
- If billing is live: keep it
- If billing is not fully live: replace the pricing section temporarily with:
  - **Header:** "Start free"
  - Copy: "We're opening access in phases. Start free and see how Canopy fits your workflow. Paid plans are coming next."

#### E. The "never shown" claim needs product/legal certainty
**Issue:** In the comparison table, "Appearance attacks" and "Sexual harassment" are labeled as never shown in any mode.

**Why update:** That's a hard promise. Hard promises should only stay if the classifier is reliable enough to defend them.

**Recommendation:**
If not fully verified, revise to:
- "Filtered by default in every mode"

That preserves the intent without making an absolute guarantee.

#### F. Testimonials are placeholders — do not ship fake social proof
**Issue:** The quotes read well, but placeholders should not go live as if real.

**Recommendation:**
- Hide the testimonial section until real beta quotes exist
- Or replace with a simple founder note / "Built with input from female creators" block
